# Writing Guide

> Source: docs/writing-guide/alpha-things-reference.md (Alpha Things)
> Last updated: 2026-05-13

---

## I. Book Positioning

**Genre**: Werewolf Dark Romance (M/M or M/F)
**Platform target**: GoodNovel / Dreame / Webnovel
**Core appeal**: Forbidden mate bond + powerful brooding Alpha + slow-burn tension + pack politics

**One-liner formula**:
> [Protagonist] never expected to be mated to [Male Lead] — the most feared/powerful/dangerous [title] alive. But the Moon Goddess doesn't make mistakes.

---

## II. Narrative Voice & POV

### First-Person Present or Past Tense (pick one, stay consistent)
Alpha Things uses **first-person past tense** for the main POV chapters, switching to **third-person limited** for backstory/prologue sections.

**✅ Do:**
- Stay deep inside the POV character's head — thoughts, instincts, wolf reactions
- Use the wolf as a second voice: `'My wolf growled in approval'` / `'My inner wolf surfaced, not liking the way he dismissed me'`
- Let the narrator be sarcastic, self-aware, and emotionally guarded

**❌ Avoid:**
- Head-hopping mid-scene
- Omniscient narration that tells the reader what other characters feel without filtering through POV

### Dual POV (recommended for romance)
Alternate chapters between the two leads. Each POV should have a distinct voice:
- **Protagonist (Lorenzo-type)**: sarcastic, guarded, reluctant, internally conflicted
- **Male Lead (Alejandro-type)**: intense, possessive, few words, actions speak louder

## III. Chapter Structure

### Opening Hook
Every chapter must open with either:
1. **Action in progress** — drop into the middle of something happening
2. **Emotional tension** — a thought or feeling that creates immediate unease
3. **Dialogue** — a line that raises a question

❌ Wrong: *"I woke up the next morning feeling refreshed."*
✅ Right: *"The blood on my hands was still warm when my father walked in."*

### Rising Action
- Each chapter = one core event + one emotional shift
- Minimum one moment of wolf instinct surfacing (growl, scent reaction, dominance display)
- Dialogue should do double duty: reveal character AND advance plot

### Ending Hook (mandatory)
Every chapter must end on one of:
- A revelation the reader didn't see coming
- An interrupted moment (kiss cut short, door knocked, phone rings)
- A decision that raises stakes
- A line of internal monologue that reframes everything

❌ Wrong: *"I went to sleep thinking about him."*
✅ Right: *"God. I'm in so much trouble."*

## IV. Pacing & Rhythm

### Word Count Per Chapter
- Target: **1,200–2,000 words** per chapter (overseas platform standard)
- Never exceed 3,000 without a major plot reason
- Short chapters = faster page-turn rate = better retention

### Sentence Rhythm
Alpha Things uses a distinctive rhythm: **long flowing sentences for emotion, short punchy sentences for action/shock.**

✅ Correct pattern:
> *"His rough large hands tracing every inch of my soft firm skin under my shirt caused my skin to burn with desire, every stroke and every rub brought goosebumps."* [long — sensory, emotional]
> *"He is a real monster."* [short — gut punch]

❌ Avoid: uniform sentence length throughout a scene (creates monotony)

### Tension Escalation
- Every 3–4 chapters: one major escalation (first touch / first kiss / first confrontation / revelation)
- Between escalations: build through small charged moments (eye contact, scent, accidental touch)
- Never resolve tension fully — always leave one thread unresolved

## V. The Mate Bond — Core Mechanic

This is the engine of the entire genre. Every scene should either:
- **Build** the mate bond (proximity, sparks, scent, involuntary reactions)
- **Resist** the mate bond (protagonist fighting it, denying it, running from it)
- **Test** the mate bond (jealousy, danger, separation)

### Physical Mate Bond Signals (use consistently)
- **Sparks**: electric sensation on skin contact — *"sparks emerge within my skin where his hand touched mine"*
- **Scent**: mate's scent described as warm, comforting, intoxicating — *"his scent surrounds me in its warmth"*
- **Eye color change**: eyes darken/go black when the wolf surfaces or desire peaks
- **Warmth**: physical warmth radiating from the mate's body
- **Heartbeat**: protagonist's heart rate spiking near the mate

### The Resistance Arc (protagonist's internal conflict)
The protagonist must have a **concrete reason** to resist the bond — not just shyness:
- Power conflict (rival for the throne)
- Identity conflict (doesn't want to be seen as weak)
- Past trauma
- External obligation (family, pack duty)

This resistance is what creates the slow burn. Without it, the story collapses in chapter 3.

## VI. Character Voice

### The Alpha Male Lead
**Core traits**: few words, intense gaze, possessive actions, controlled rage, hidden vulnerability
**Speech pattern**: short declarative sentences, commands, rare moments of softness that hit harder because of their rarity

✅ Right:
> *"You are my Mate Lorenzo, there is no denying it, get this drilled in your thick head that you will always be my mate."*
> *"Not for long though"* → *"shut up"* [cuts off, doesn't argue — acts]

❌ Wrong: long explanations, justifying himself, being verbally expressive about feelings

**The vulnerability reveal**: must be earned, not given early. Alpha Things waits until Ch.16 for Alejandro's first real emotional crack (*"I'm not like him"*). This is the payoff for 15 chapters of buildup.

### The Protagonist (POV character)
**Core traits**: competent, sarcastic, emotionally guarded, secretly drawn to the mate
**Internal monologue**: frequent, self-aware, often contradicts their outward behavior

✅ Right:
> *"I snort at him and he looks startled at me"* — protagonist acts unbothered outwardly
> *"my heart beating a mile, the dryness in my throat resembles the Sahara desert"* — internally falling apart

This gap between external behavior and internal state is the primary source of tension and reader engagement.

### Supporting Characters
- **Best friend / Beta**: comic relief + loyalty + occasional wisdom. Speaks plainly, calls out the protagonist's BS
- **Antagonist**: must have a comprehensible motivation, not just be evil. Alpha Things uses the rival Alpha whose father wants the throne — personal history with the protagonist

## VII. Werewolf World-Building Rules

### Show, Don't Explain
Introduce pack hierarchy, mate bonds, and wolf abilities through **action and reaction**, not exposition dumps.

❌ Wrong: *"In werewolf society, the Alpha is the most powerful member of the pack. The mate bond is a sacred connection created by the Moon Goddess..."*
✅ Right: *"He stood down the moment Alejandro's Alpha power rolled off him in waves — even Sorin, his closest friend, couldn't resist it."*

### Hierarchy to establish early (Ch.1–3)
- Alpha > Beta > Omega
- Pureblood vs. mixed blood
- The Moon Goddess as the ultimate authority (fate, mate bonds, prophecy)
- Pack loyalty as a core value — betrayal is the worst sin

### The Wolf as a Character
The inner wolf is a **separate entity** with its own reactions, desires, and opinions. Use it to:
- Create internal conflict (*"my wolf growled in protest but I pushed him down"*)
- Signal danger before the protagonist consciously registers it
- Express what the protagonist won't admit (*"my wolf howled in joy — I told him to shut up"*)

## VIII. Dialogue Rules

### Less is more
Alpha Things' most powerful dialogue moments are the shortest ones.
> *"You are my Mate."* — 4 words. No elaboration needed.

### Dialogue tags
Use **action beats** instead of adverb-heavy tags:
❌ *"I love you," he said softly.*
✅ *"I love you." He didn't look at me when he said it.*

### Subtext
Characters rarely say exactly what they mean. Layer meaning:
- What they say vs. what they do
- What they say vs. what the POV character interprets
- What they don't say (silence as dialogue)

## IX. Sensory & Physical Description

### The Alpha's Physical Description
Establish early, reference consistently. Alpha Things' formula:
- Height (always tall, 6ft+)
- Eyes (color that changes with emotion — blue/grey, goes black)
- Build (described through action: *"his abs glistening with sweat, his stance alert, his large arms forward"*)
- Scent (warm, dark, specific — pine / rain / cedar / musk)

### Touch Scenes
Build physical intimacy in layers:
1. Accidental touch → sparks
2. Deliberate proximity → tension
3. First intentional touch (hand, face)
4. First kiss (interrupted or cut short)
5. Full intimacy (much later, after emotional bond is established)

Never skip layers. The payoff depends on the buildup.

---

## X. Chapter Checklist

Before finishing any chapter:
- [ ] Opens with a hook (action / tension / dialogue)
- [ ] Contains at least one wolf instinct moment
- [ ] Mate bond is either built, resisted, or tested
- [ ] Dialogue reveals character, not just information
- [ ] Sentence rhythm varies (long + short)
- [ ] Ends on a hook or unresolved tension
- [ ] POV is consistent throughout
- [ ] No exposition dumps — world-building through action
- [ ] Word count 1,200–2,000
- [ ] Alpha male lead speaks in short, direct sentences
- [ ] Protagonist's internal state contradicts their external behavior (at least once)
