# Chapter 9 — Proximity

The room he arranged was next to his.

I noticed this immediately. I didn't say anything about it, because saying something about it would require acknowledging that I'd noticed, and I wasn't ready to do that.

The room was larger than anywhere I'd slept in the past two weeks — a real bed, a window that faced east, a fireplace that someone had already lit. I set my bag down and stood in the middle of it and felt the bond settle into something almost comfortable now that we were on the same side of a wall.

My wolf approved. Loudly.

*Don't,* I told her.

She ignored me.

---

I didn't sleep well. Not because of the room — the room was fine — but because the bond was very present and the wall between us was not very thick and I was acutely aware of the direction in a way that had nothing to do with compass points and everything to do with the fact that he was approximately fifteen feet away.

I lay in the dark and thought about his hand on mine. The barely-there contact. The way his breath had gone shallow.

*He feels it too,* my wolf said.

*I know,* I told her.

*He's been feeling it since the Moon Ball.*

*I know.*

*He rejected it to protect you.*

*I know that too. It doesn't change anything.*

She was quiet for a moment. Then: *Doesn't it?*

I didn't answer her. I stared at the ceiling and listened to the palace settle around me and thought about the binding and Cassius and the thirty people in the hills and all the reasons this was not the time to think about the way his knuckles had gone white.

---

He knocked on my door at dawn.

I was already dressed — I'd given up on sleep around the third hour and spent the rest of the time going over the book again, committing the binding words to memory. I opened the door and he was standing there in the corridor looking like he hadn't slept either, which I found unreasonably satisfying.

*"Cassius has requested an audience,"* he said. *"This morning."*

*"How long do we have?"*

*"An hour. Maybe less."*

I stepped back from the door. *"Come in."*

He came in. The room felt smaller with him in it — not because he was large, though he was, but because the bond adjusted to proximity the way a flame adjusts to wind, burning hotter and more directed. I moved to the desk and put it between us, which helped approximately nothing.

*"The binding,"* I said. *"I've memorized the words."*

*"You can't speak it yet. The bond has to be accepted first."*

*"I know. So we need to accept it before Cassius gets to you."*

He looked at me across the desk. His expression was careful, controlled — the same expression he'd worn in the throne room when he'd said the four words. But his hands were at his sides and his fingers were not entirely still.

*"If I accept it,"* he said, *"it's not reversible."*

*"I know."*

*"You'd be bound to me. Permanently."*

*"I know what a mate bond is."*

*"This one is different. Constitutional. It changes what I am — what we both are."*

*"I know,"* I said, for the third time. *"I read the book."*

He was quiet. The bond was very loud in the silence — a heat, a pull, a pressure that had been building since the Moon Ball and had been getting worse every time we were in the same room. I could feel my pulse in my throat. I could feel the warmth of him from six feet away.

*"I rejected it to protect you,"* he said. *"I want you to know that."*

*"I know,"* I said. *"I figured that out."*

Something shifted in his expression — not quite surprise, but close to it. Like he'd expected to have to explain more.

*"It didn't work,"* I said. *"I'm here anyway."*

*"Yes,"* he said. *"You are."*

The way he said it — quiet, direct, with something underneath it that he wasn't naming — made my wolf go very still and my pulse go very loud.

*"We have an hour,"* I said. *"How do we do this?"*

---

He crossed the room.

Not fast — deliberately, giving me time to step back if I was going to. I didn't step back. He stopped in front of me, close enough that I could feel the warmth of him, close enough that the bond was a full-body pressure, heat spreading from my sternum outward, my breath going shallow.

He raised his hand and touched my face — his palm against my jaw, careful, like something he'd been not-doing for a long time.

The bond flared.

Not the fist-to-sternum of the Moon Ball — something deeper, something that went all the way down, a resonance that felt like the tuning fork and the book and the hairpin all at once. My wolf made a sound I had no word for. My hand came up and gripped his wrist — not to pull away, just to hold on.

*"Accepted,"* he said quietly.

The word landed in my chest like a key turning.

The bond settled. Not gone — more present than ever, actually, but different. Steady. Like something that had been straining against a wall had finally been allowed to simply exist.

I was still holding his wrist. His pulse was fast under my fingers.

*"Accepted,"* I said.

He didn't move his hand from my face.

Neither of us moved for a long moment.

Then someone knocked on the door and said, *"Your Majesty. Lord Cassius is here."*

---

*One hour. They'd used every second of it.*
