# Chapter 7 — Cassius Moves

The note was under my door when I got back.

No envelope. No seal. Just a folded piece of paper with two lines on it, written in that careful, precise hand I now recognized on sight.

*Miss Voss. I know you're here. We should speak before you do something irreversible.*

I stood in the doorway and read it twice.

He'd found me in under a week. Which meant he'd been watching for me — not just in the capital, but on the roads, at the inns. He'd had people looking. He'd known I might come back.

He'd underestimated me at the Moon Ball. He wasn't making that mistake twice.

I looked around the room. Nothing disturbed. The four remaining coins were still in the pouch inside my shirt. I packed in four minutes. I was out the side door in five.

---

The second inn was smaller, further into the outer district. I paid cash, gave a different false name, and sat in the dark thinking about what the note meant.

Not the words. The meaning underneath.

*We should speak before you do something irreversible.*

He wasn't threatening me. He was warning me. Which meant he thought I was about to do something bad for me, not just inconvenient for him. Which meant he thought I was going to go to Kael.

He was right. I already had.

But the warning implied something else: that going to Kael was dangerous for me specifically. Cassius had spent thirty-one years managing this situation. He'd massacred an entire bloodline to manage it. He wasn't going to let a warning note be his only move.

I thought through his options the way he would — methodically, without sentiment.

He could discredit me. I was a border pack nobody with no documentation and a false name. If I walked into the palace claiming to be the last of a massacred bloodline with evidence against the Lord Chancellor, the system would remove me as a nuisance without him lifting a finger.

He could remove me quietly. He'd tried the graceful version at the Moon Ball. The less graceful version was available to a man of his resources.

The third option was the most elegant: get to Kael first. Tell him whatever story made me the problem rather than the solution. Kael had already rejected the bond — Cassius just needed to make sure he kept rejecting it, and kept rejecting me along with it.

Option three closed the door permanently.

I needed to move faster than he expected.

---

I sent a message to Kael through Mira's network before dawn. Three lines: *Cassius knows I'm here. He'll move against me directly. I need the vault.*

His reply came back in an hour. A folded note, four words and a seal pressed in wax.

*Give this to Aldric.*

---

The steward's name was Aldric, and he had forty years of palace service and the particular quality of very old staff: loyalty to the institution rather than to any individual within it. He read Kael's note, looked at me, looked at the hairpin, and said nothing for a long moment.

Then: *"Follow me."*

The vault was underground. Stone walls, careful shelving, objects wrapped in cloth and labeled in a hand that had changed several times over the decades. Aldric led me to a section near the back.

*"These came in thirty-one years ago,"* he said. *"The order was unusual. No provenance documentation. Just: store these, seal the record."*

*"Cassius's order?"*

He looked at me. *"I was told not to ask."*

He left me with a lamp and the instruction to be gone before the night guard's second round.

---

There were eleven objects. I unwrapped each one carefully.

Most I couldn't identify — small things, worn smooth, the kind of objects that accumulate in a community over generations. A carved wooden disc. A length of braided cord. A small clay figure, features blurred with age.

The seventh object was a book.

Small — the size of my hand, bound in something that wasn't quite leather. The pages were thin, the writing inside in a script I didn't recognize. But the cover had a symbol pressed into it: a leaf, narrow and elongated.

The same shape as the hairpin.

My wolf went completely still.

I held the book up to the lamp. On the back cover, in a different hand — newer ink, less faded — someone had written four lines in the common tongue.

*For the one who comes after.*
*The bond is not a chain.*
*The blood remembers what the mind forgets.*
*The pin opens what the key cannot.*

I read it three times.

*The pin opens what the key cannot.*

I touched the hairpin to the cover.

The symbol lit — not light, exactly, but warmth, a resonance, the way a tuning fork responds to its frequency. The book fell open to a page near the middle, and the script on that page was different: cleaner, more recent, written in the common tongue.

*If you are reading this, the bloodline survived. What follows is what Cassius Hale does not want you to know.*

---

*She had two hours. She started reading.*
