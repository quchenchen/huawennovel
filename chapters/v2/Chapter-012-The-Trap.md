# Chapter 12 — The Trap

The convening was set for the seventh hour.

I spent the afternoon in my room going over the binding words until they were as automatic as breathing. Four words in the old tongue — the book had included a phonetic guide, and I'd read it until the sounds were muscle memory. I wasn't going to stumble. I wasn't going to hesitate.

At the sixth hour, Mira knocked on my door.

Her face was wrong.

*"What happened?"* I asked.

*"The vault,"* she said. *"Aldric reported it. The book — it's been logged as missing. Cassius filed a theft report an hour ago."* She looked at me. *"He named you."*

I sat down on the edge of the bed.

Of course he had. He'd known I'd taken it — Aldric had seen the bag, and Aldric served the institution, and Cassius had thirty years of institutional relationships. He'd waited until one hour before the convening, which was exactly enough time to have me detained and not enough time for Kael to sort it out before the council assembled.

*"Where's Kael?"* I asked.

*"With the council. They've already been told."*

*"And?"*

She hesitated. *"Lord Cassius is requesting that you be held pending investigation. The council is — they're divided. But the theft report is formal. Kael can't simply ignore it."*

I thought about what Cassius had said at the window. *I have allies on every council.*

He'd planned this. Not as a backup — as the plan. The warning had been a test: if I left, problem solved. If I stayed, he'd use the book against me. Either way, I didn't speak the binding.

*"I need to talk to Kael,"* I said.

*"He's asking for you,"* Mira said. *"That's why I came."*

---

Kael was in the antechamber off the council room, alone. He looked at me when I came in and his expression was controlled — the throne room expression, the one that gave nothing away.

But his hands were at his sides and his fingers were not still.

*"The theft report,"* I said.

*"Yes."*

*"He planned it."*

*"I know."*

*"If you detain me, the convening gets delayed. He gets time to build his case, discredit the book, discredit me."*

*"I know,"* he said again. *"Sera."*

He'd used my name. Not *Miss Voss.* My name.

*"I have to respond to the report,"* he said. *"If I don't, I'm seen as protecting you over the law. Cassius will use that — he'll say the bond has compromised my judgment."*

*"Has it?"*

He looked at me for a long moment. *"No,"* he said. *"But perception matters. I need you to trust me."*

*"Trust you to do what?"*

*"To detain you formally — in the palace, not a cell, with full access to the convening — and then to convene anyway. Tonight. As planned."*

I thought about that. *"He'll object."*

*"He'll object to everything. That's not new."* His jaw was set. *"The binding doesn't require you to be free. It requires you to speak. You can speak from anywhere in the throne room."*

I looked at him. The bond was steady between us — warm, present, the thing that had settled when he'd said *accepted* and hadn't unsettled since.

*"If this goes wrong,"* I said.

*"It won't."*

*"If it does."*

*"Then I'll deal with the consequences,"* he said. *"That's what the binding is for. Making me answerable."*

Something in my chest did something I wasn't going to examine right now.

*"Fine,"* I said. *"Detain me. But Kael—"*

*"Yes?"*

*"If you say the four words again, I will make your life very difficult."*

Something shifted in his expression — not quite a smile, but the thing that happens in a face just before a smile.

*"Noted,"* he said.

---

The formal detention lasted forty-seven minutes. I spent it in the antechamber with Mira and the binding words and the knowledge that Cassius was somewhere in the palace recalculating.

At the seventh hour, the council assembled.

At the seventh hour and three minutes, Kael walked me into the throne room himself.

Cassius was already there. He saw us come in together and his hands, for the first time, were not folded.

*"Your Majesty,"* he said. *"The detained party shouldn't be—"*

*"The convening is called,"* Kael said. *"Sit down, Cassius."*

A beat. Two beats.

Cassius sat down.

I walked to the center of the room.

---

*Forty-seven minutes. Then she walked into the throne room and the thirty-one years of silence ended.*
