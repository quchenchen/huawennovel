# Chapter 13 — Speak the Binding

The throne room was full.

Kael's full council — twelve people, seated along the walls. Three senior archivists, called as witnesses. Two representatives from the palace guard. And Cassius, at the far end of the room, with his hands folded on the table in front of him and his expression perfectly composed.

I stood in the center of the room and looked at him.

He looked back at me. He smiled.

*He still thinks he can manage this,* I thought. *Good. Let him think that.*

Kael took his seat at the head of the room. *"This convening is called under the old compact,"* he said. *"A bonded Vael has requested the right to speak the binding. The compact requires that I hear it."* He paused. *"Miss Voss."*

I took a breath.

The binding words were four syllables in the old tongue. The book had given me the phonetics, and I'd practiced them until they were automatic, and I said them now clearly and without hesitation, and the moment the last syllable left my mouth something happened in the room.

Not dramatic. Not light or sound or any of the things the stories would have you expect. Just a quality of attention — a stillness, like the room itself was listening. Like something very old had been waiting for this and had just been told it could proceed.

Cassius's hands, for the first time, were not folded.

*"The binding is spoken,"* Kael said. His voice was even. *"The compact is invoked. I am required to hear testimony and render judgment."* He looked at me. *"Speak."*

---

I spoke.

I started with what I'd found in the archive — the census record, the notation *relocated*, supplementary record 7-C. And then I recited it. Word for word, the way I'd memorized it in the vault by lamplight, the way I'd gone over it every day since.

*"Re: the hill settlements, Ashvale district. Relocation to be completed before the winter solstice. No record of origin to be retained in public documents. Supplementary records to be sealed for fifty years."*

The room was very quiet.

*"The handwriting,"* I said, *"matches the handwriting on the border pack's formal introduction letter, which was prepared by Lord Cassius Hale and which I handled personally during the Moon Ball delegation."*

I looked at Cassius.

*"I also testify that Lord Cassius Hale knew my name before the Moon Ball, had prepared forty-three gold coins for my departure before the bond was identified, and that his response when I agreed to leave was relief — not at my departure, but at the confirmation that I was moving where he'd planned."*

I paused.

*"The Silver Vael were not relocated. They were killed. Thirty people, in the hills above Ashvale, one winter thirty-one years ago. My mother was the only survivor. She spent twenty years hiding at the border pack to keep me alive."* I looked at the council, not at Cassius. *"I am the last of the Silver Vael. I am bonded to the Alpha King under the old compact. And I am asking this council to hear what I've said and hold it on record."*

Silence.

Then Cassius said, *"This is fabrication."* His voice was perfectly calm. *"The girl has a grudge and a story. The 'order' she recited doesn't exist — I challenge anyone to find it in the archive."*

*"It's been sealed for fifty years,"* I said. *"By your own order. It won't be accessible for another nineteen years."*

*"Convenient,"* he said.

*"Yes,"* I agreed. *"It was. For you."*

---

*"Lord Cassius."*

Kael's voice. Quiet, even, the voice he used when he wasn't going to repeat himself.

Cassius looked at him.

*"I have heard the testimony,"* Kael said. *"I have heard your denial. The compact requires my judgment."* A pause. *"I am also a witness. I knew my father authorized actions through you that were never formally recorded. I chose not to examine them closely."* His voice didn't change. *"I am examining them now."*

Something moved in Cassius's expression. The first real crack — not the flash I'd seen at the window, but something deeper, something that had been held in place for thirty-one years and was starting to give.

*"You have no proof,"* Cassius said.

*"No,"* Kael said. *"But I have the compact. And the compact requires me to act on testimony spoken under the binding, in the absence of contradicting evidence."* He looked at Cassius steadily. *"You have thirty years of service. You have allies in this room. You have documentation for every decision you've ever made."* A pause. *"Show me the documentation for the hill settlements."*

Silence.

*"Show me the documentation,"* Kael said again, *"for the relocation of the Silver Vael."*

The silence stretched.

Cassius's hands were flat on the table. Not folded. Flat, pressed down, like he was holding something in place.

*"There is no documentation,"* he said finally. *"Because there was no relocation."*

*"Then what happened to them?"*

The room held its breath.

---

*He couldn't lie. Not here. Not to this. The compact didn't allow it.*
