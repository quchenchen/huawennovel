# Chapter 6 — The Garden

Getting to Kael Dravon required Mira.

She was a palace attendant I'd met during the Moon Ball delegation — young, efficient, the kind of person who knew how everything worked and was invisible because of it. She'd been kind to me in the way people are kind when they don't expect to see you again. I'd remembered her name.

I found her at the market near the palace gates the next morning. She looked at me with the expression of someone trying to place a face they hadn't expected to see again.

*"Miss Voss,"* she said, when it clicked.

*"Sera. And I need a way in that doesn't go through official channels."*

She looked at me for a long moment. Then she looked at the hairpin — the same way Maren had looked at it, the same way the fence-mender had. Something shifted in her expression.

*"Come with me,"* she said. *"Not here."*

---

In a small room off the palace kitchens, I told her enough. Not everything — not the archive, not the order. But the shape: who I was, what I'd found, why I needed to speak to the King before someone else did.

She listened. When I finished, she was quiet.

*"He doesn't take audiences,"* she said. *"Not for the past three years."*

*"I'm not asking for an audience. I'm asking for a way in that he won't see coming."*

*"That's more dangerous than an audience."*

*"I know."*

She looked at the hairpin again. Something in her expression settled.

*"There's a garden,"* she said slowly. *"East side of the palace. He walks there in the evenings. Alone — he insists on it. The guards do a perimeter, but the garden itself is unwatched."*

*"Why are you telling me this?"*

*"Because my grandmother was from the hill settlements above Ashvale,"* she said. *"And she told me, before she died, that if I ever met someone wearing a silver leaf pin, I should help them."* A pause. *"She didn't explain why. She just said: help them."*

She gave me the gate, the time, the path.

*"Be careful,"* she said. *"He's not easy to talk to. He hasn't been, for a long time."*

---

The garden was old, the kind of old that happens when something has been tended for generations and then left to find its own shape. I arrived early and sat on a bench near the far wall and waited.

My wolf was very still. Not danger-still. Something else.

He came at dusk.

I heard him before I saw him — not footsteps, but the quality of silence that moves with someone used to being the most significant thing in a room. He came around the bend in the path and stopped when he saw me.

He went very still.

Not surprise. The stillness of someone who had been waiting for something and had just seen it arrive.

We looked at each other across the garden path. He was taller than I remembered. He looked tired in a way that had nothing to do with sleep — the kind of tired that settles into a person over years and becomes structural. His eyes were dark, careful, giving nothing away.

Except that they were giving something away right now.

*"Miss Voss,"* he said.

*"You know my name,"* I said. *"I've been wondering about that."*

*"Cassius told me. After the Moon Ball."* A pause. *"Tell me what you found."*

He sat down across from me. Not *leave.* Not the four words again. He sat down and waited.

---

I told him. The Vael, the archive, the order in Cassius's handwriting. The thirty-one years. The artifacts in the vault.

He listened without interrupting. His hands were resting on his knees when I started. By the time I finished they were very still, the kind of still that takes effort.

*"The order,"* he said. *"You saw it."*

*"I memorized it."*

*"He'll deny it. He's had thirty years to build the story he'd tell if this surfaced."*

*"I know. That's not why I came to you."* I looked at him. *"You hesitated. At the Moon Ball. You looked at me and something shifted and then you said the four words. You knew something about what I was."*

The silence stretched.

*"Yes,"* he said finally.

He told me in pieces. The bond between a Vael and an Alpha King was not a standard mate bond — his father had told him this, years ago, in the way kings tell their heirs things they hope will never be relevant. The Vael were old. Their blood carried something that interacted with an Alpha King's authority in a way that was structural rather than personal. A Vael bond didn't just connect two people. It changed what the Alpha King was.

*"Changed how?"* I asked.

*"Made him answerable,"* he said. *"To something other than himself."*

*"That sounds like something a Lord Chancellor would want to prevent."*

*"Yes."*

He was quiet. Then: *"Cassius told me you were a border pack nobody with a fabricated bloodline claim. That the bond had misfired. He said the kindest thing was to reject it cleanly and let you go."*

*"And you believed him."*

*"I wanted to believe him."*

That landed differently than I expected. I looked at him — the structural tiredness, the careful eyes — and thought about what Mira had said. *He hasn't been easy to talk to, for a long time.*

*"Your mate,"* I said. *"Three years ago."*

His expression didn't change. But something in the air around him did.

*"Cassius,"* I said. Not a question.

A long silence.

*"I don't have proof,"* he said finally.

*"I know what that's like,"* I said.

We sat with that. Two people with the shape of something and no way to make it stick.

*"The bond,"* I said. *"You rejected it. But it's still there."*

*"I know."*

*"Does it — for you, does it—"*

*"Yes,"* he said. *"It does."*

He said it directly, without deflection, and looked at the garden instead of at me, and his jaw was set in the way people set their jaws when saying something costs them something.

*"I rejected it because the last time I accepted a bond, Cassius used it against me,"* he said. *"I rejected it because I thought you were what he said you were. And I rejected it because I didn't want to be responsible for another person's safety."* A pause. *"I was wrong about all three. I knew I was wrong about at least one of them before you left the throne room."*

The garden was very quiet.

*"So,"* I said. *"We both have the shape of something and no proof."*

*"Yes."*

*"And Cassius is going to move against me directly if I stay."*

*"Yes."*

*"Then we have a problem,"* I said. *"And I think we're going to have to solve it together."*

He looked at me then — the full weight of it.

*"Yes,"* he said. *"I think we are."*

The bond was very present between us. Not pulling — just there, like a held breath.

Neither of us moved.

---

*She'd come to the garden for information. She was leaving with an alliance. And something else she wasn't naming yet.*
