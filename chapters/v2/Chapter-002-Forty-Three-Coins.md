# Chapter 2 — Forty-Three Coins

Generous people give round numbers.

I sat in the carriage and counted again.

Forty-three.

Not forty. Not fifty.

Someone had calculated exactly how much it cost to make me disappear.

I put the coins back. Pressed the envelope flat. Tucked it under my bag.

Then I told the driver to stop.

---

He looked at me. The route in the envelope said border. That was where I was supposed to go.

I looked back at him until he stopped looking at me.

*"I'll arrange my own transport from here,"* I said.

He didn't argue.

Cassius had chosen well. The man had been paid to deliver me, not to make sure I stayed delivered.

---

The inn was small. One night. Two coins.

I sat on the edge of the bed with the knife from my boot in my hand.

Not because I expected trouble.

Because holding it helped me think.

The bond was still there. Northwest. Steady. A compass needle fixed on a direction I wasn't going.

My wolf was pacing.

*We're not going back,* I told her.

She didn't agree.

She was going to keep not agreeing.

---

I thought about my mother.

Almost nothing. She died when I was six. My father never spoke of her. She'd come from somewhere east — she'd mentioned it once, something about the light in the mornings.

She'd had no status when she arrived at the border pack. No pack affiliation. Nothing except a child and a silver hairpin she wore every day until she didn't.

The hairpin was in my hair right now.

I reached up and took it out.

Small. Silver. Shaped like a narrow leaf. Worn smooth from years of handling — mine, and before mine, hers.

I held it up to the candle.

The light caught it wrong.

Not the flat gleam of ordinary silver. Something deeper. Like the metal was holding the light rather than reflecting it.

My wolf went completely still.

*What?* I asked her.

She didn't answer.

She just stayed still.

Which was its own kind of answer.

---

I put the hairpin back. Blew out the candle. Lay down with the knife under the pillow.

The bond pulled northwest.

I was going east.

My mother had come from somewhere. Cassius had known my name before we met. He'd had forty-three coins ready before the bond was identified.

Three things that didn't fit.

That was enough.

---

*She didn't sleep.*

*She was already planning.*
