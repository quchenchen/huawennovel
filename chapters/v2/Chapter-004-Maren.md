# Chapter 4 — Maren

She lived at the edge of town in a house that had decided to become part of the hillside. Stone walls, a low roof, a garden gone wild in the way gardens go wild when someone has stopped caring about tidiness and started caring only about what grows.

The woman at the bakery had told me: *"You want Maren. She's the only one left who'll say anything."*

Maren was sitting in her garden when I arrived, doing nothing in particular. She looked at me the way the fence-mender had — checking something against a memory.

She didn't look at my face first.

She looked at the hairpin.

*"I wondered,"* she said, *"if one of you would come eventually."*

I stayed very still. *"One of who?"*

*"Sit down,"* she said. *"You've been walking since dawn."*

I sat.

---

*"Your mother was Lena Vael,"* she said. *"Before she was Lena Voss. Before she ran."*

*"Vael,"* I repeated.

*"The Silver Vael."* She said it the way you say a name that used to mean something. *"That was what they were called, in the hills. The ones who carried the old blood."*

My wolf went very still.

*"What old blood?"* I asked.

*"The kind that doesn't forget what it is,"* Maren said, *"even when the people carrying it do."*

She told me what she knew — not everything, because she didn't know everything. She knew what her mother had told her, and her mother had known what she'd seen, and what she'd seen was the end of it.

The Silver Vael had lived in the hills above Ashvale for as long as anyone could remember. They were wolves, but something older ran in their blood — something structural, something that had been there so long it had become part of what they were. They had a particular relationship with silver that no other wolves had.

*"Silver doesn't burn them,"* Maren said. *"It never did. That's how you knew."*

I thought about the hairpin. About wearing it my whole life without thinking about it. About the way other wolves flinched from silver and I never had. I'd thought I was just less sensitive.

*"What happened to them?"* I asked.

*"One winter,"* Maren said. *"About thirty years ago. Gone by spring."*

*"Not an accident,"* I said.

She looked at me sharply. *"Who told you that?"*

*"Someone at the market."*

She was quiet. *"No. Not an accident. Someone with enough reach to make it happen quietly and enough interest in keeping it quiet afterward."* A pause. *"Your mother was the only one who got out. She was barely more than a girl. She ran south and didn't stop until she was somewhere no one would think to look."*

*"The border pack,"* I said.

*"The border pack."*

---

I took the hairpin out of my hair and held it up to the morning light.

The silver glow. The way it held the light rather than reflected it.

*"Is this—"*

*"Yes,"* Maren said, before I finished. *"It's old. Older than your mother, older than her mother. It's been in the Vael line a long time."* She paused. *"It's not just silver. But I don't know what else it is. That knowledge went with the ones in the hills."*

My wolf made a sound I'd never heard from her before — low, resonant, something between grief and recognition.

I closed my fingers around the hairpin.

*"If you have that pin,"* Maren said, *"you're the last one."*

She said it simply. The way you state a fact that has been true for a long time.

I sat with it anyway.

*The last one.*

---

Maren stood up slowly. *"I've told you what I know. The rest — why they were killed, who ordered it, what the Vael blood actually means — that's not in this valley anymore."* She looked at me. *"If you want those answers, you'll have to go to the capital. The old records. Someone kept records — someone always does. And whoever ordered it thirty years ago is probably still there."*

She paused.

*"Be careful,"* she said. *"Your mother was careful too. It wasn't enough."*

She went inside and closed the door.

I stood in her garden for a moment. The bond pulled northwest. The capital was northwest.

I'd been going east specifically to avoid going back.

I pinned the hairpin back in my hair.

*Fine,* I told my wolf. *Northwest.*

She didn't say *I told you so.* She didn't have to.

---

*The last one. Fine. Then she'd be the last one who did something about it.*
