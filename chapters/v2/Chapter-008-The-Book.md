# Chapter 8 — The Book

The common-tongue section was only a few pages. Written by someone who had known they were running out of time.

I read it twice, standing in the vault with the lamp burning low, and by the time I finished I understood why Cassius had killed an entire bloodline to keep this buried.

The Vael were not just a bloodline with silver immunity. They were the original witnesses — the ones who had, generations ago, formalized the compact between the Alpha King's authority and something older than the kingdom. The bond between a Vael and an Alpha King was not romantic in origin. It was constitutional. A Vael bonded to the King could *speak the binding* — four words in the old tongue — and require him to answer for actions taken against the compact's terms. Publicly. On record. With no way to refuse.

Cassius had ordered the massacre because a Vael had been preparing to speak the binding against the previous king — Kael's father — for actions Cassius had taken in the king's name. Unauthorized actions. Actions that, if the binding had been spoken, would have been laid at the king's feet and required answer.

He'd killed thirty people to prevent four words.

And then he'd spent thirty-one years making sure no one was left who could speak them.

Except me.

I wrapped the book back in its cloth and put it in my bag. I knew I was taking something that wasn't mine. I did it anyway, because leaving it here meant Cassius would find it and destroy it the moment he realized what I'd found.

Aldric saw the bag when I came out. He didn't ask.

---

I brought the book to Kael.

He read the common-tongue section without speaking. When he finished, he set it down on the garden bench between us and looked at the wall for a long moment.

*"My father,"* he said finally.

*"Cassius acted in his name,"* I said. *"Without authorization. The Vael were going to expose it."*

*"And my father let Cassius handle it."*

I didn't answer that. I didn't need to.

He picked up the book again. Turned it over in his hands. *"The binding,"* he said. *"You can speak it."*

*"According to this, yes. A bonded Vael."* I paused. *"Which requires the bond to be accepted."*

He looked at me then.

The bond was very present — it always was when we were this close, a heat across my sternum, a pull I'd been managing for two weeks. His jaw was set. His hands were still on the book, but his knuckles had gone white.

*"I rejected it,"* he said.

*"I know."*

*"A rejection doesn't dissolve it. Not immediately."*

*"I know that too."*

We looked at each other. The garden was quiet around us, the evening light going gold.

*"If I accept it,"* he said slowly, *"Cassius will use it against you. The way he used—"* He stopped.

*"The way he used your mate's bond against you,"* I said.

His expression didn't change. But something in his hands did — the knuckles went whiter.

*"Yes,"* he said.

*"He's already moving against me,"* I said. *"The note under my door. He knows I'm here, he knows I've been to the archive, and he's going to move before I can do anything with what I found. Accepting the bond doesn't make me more of a target. I'm already the target."*

He was quiet for a long time.

The bond pulled between us — not metaphorically, physically, a warmth that had been building since I sat down, since he sat down, since we were close enough that I could feel the heat of him at two feet of distance. My wolf was not pacing. She was very, very still, the way she gets when something is about to be decided.

*"If I accept it,"* he said, *"and you speak the binding — what happens to Cassius?"*

*"He has to answer. Publicly. For the unauthorized actions, for the massacre, for all of it. On record."*

*"And if he moves against you before you can speak it?"*

*"Then we lose,"* I said. *"But we're losing anyway if we do nothing."*

He looked at me for a long moment. Then he reached out and touched the back of my hand — just his fingers, barely contact, the lightest possible touch — and the bond flared so hard I stopped breathing.

He felt it too. I could see it in the way his jaw tightened, the way his breath went shallow.

He didn't move his hand.

*"I need a day,"* he said. *"To prepare. If we're going to do this, we do it right."*

*"Cassius might not give us a day."*

*"I know."* His fingers were still on the back of my hand. *"Stay in the palace tonight. I'll arrange a room."*

*"That's—"*

*"Safer,"* he said. *"And I'm not asking."*

I looked at his hand on mine. The bond was a full-body heat now, my pulse loud in my ears, my wolf making a sound that was nothing like grief and nothing like warning.

*"Fine,"* I said. *"One night."*

He moved his hand. The absence of contact was almost worse than the contact had been.

---

*He'd touched her hand. That was all. She was going to be thinking about it for a very long time.*
