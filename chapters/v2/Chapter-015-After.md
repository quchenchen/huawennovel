# Chapter 15 — After

The council dispersed slowly, in the way councils disperse after something significant — not all at once, but in clusters, people stopping to speak quietly to each other, to look at the document Cassius had left on the table, to look at me.

I let them look. I was too tired to manage impressions.

Mira found me near the door and pressed a cup of something warm into my hands and didn't say anything, which was exactly right. I drank it. It was tea. I hadn't eaten since morning.

*"The review will take weeks,"* Kael said, appearing beside me. *"Possibly months. His allies will fight it."*

*"I know."*

*"The testimony is on record. The compact holds it. That doesn't change regardless of what the review finds."*

*"I know."*

He was quiet for a moment. *"You should eat something."*

*"I know that too."*

The not-quite-smile. *"You know a great deal."*

*"I've been told."*

We stood there in the emptying throne room, and the bond was warm between us, and I thought about my mother running south with a silver hairpin and a child and no plan except to survive. I thought about Maren saying *she was careful* and *it wasn't enough.* I thought about thirty people in the hills who had been called *relocated* in a census record.

The testimony was on record now. Their name was on record. *Silver Vael.* Not *relocated.* Not *dispersed.* Their name, spoken under the binding, held by the compact.

It wasn't enough. It wasn't justice. But it was a start.

*"The Vael history,"* I said. *"It should be formally recognized. Not just in the compact record — in the public record. What happened to them."*

*"Yes,"* Kael said. *"It should."*

*"Will you do that?"*

He looked at me. *"Is that a request or a binding requirement?"*

*"A request,"* I said. *"For now."*

*"Then yes,"* he said. *"I'll do that."*

---

We ate in a small room off the kitchens — Kael, Mira, and me, which was not how I'd imagined the evening ending. Mira produced bread and cold meat and something that might have been soup, and we ate it without ceremony, and it was the most normal thing that had happened to me in three weeks.

Kael ate like someone who had forgotten to eat for most of the day, which I found unreasonably humanizing.

*"What happens now?"* I asked. *"To me, specifically."*

*"That depends,"* he said.

*"On what?"*

He looked at me across the table. The bond was very present — warm, steady, the thing that had settled when he'd said *accepted* and had been settling further ever since, like something finding its level.

*"On what you want,"* he said.

I thought about that. *"I want the Vael history recognized. I want the supplementary records unsealed — all of them, not just 7-C. I want Maren to know that what she told me mattered."*

*"Those are things I can do,"* he said. *"What do you want for yourself?"*

I looked at him. *"I'm not going back to the border pack."*

*"No,"* he agreed. *"You're not."*

*"I don't have a plan beyond that."*

*"You could stay,"* he said. *"Here. In the capital."* A pause. *"There's a role — formal, with a title, for the bonded Vael under the compact. It's been empty for thirty-one years."*

*"A title,"* I said.

*"Yes."*

*"What kind of title?"*

*"The kind that means you can walk into any room in this kingdom and be heard,"* he said. *"The kind that means Cassius's allies can't dismiss you as a border pack nobody."*

I thought about that for a long moment.

*"I'll think about it,"* I said.

*"That's not a no,"* Mira said, from the other end of the table.

*"No,"* I agreed. *"It's not."*

Kael looked at me with the expression I was starting to recognize — the one that appeared when I'd done something he hadn't expected, or said something that landed differently than he'd prepared for. He looked away first, which was new.

My wolf made the smug sound again.

*Don't,* I told her.

She didn't listen. She never listens when she's right.

---

*She kept the hairpin. She always would. But she was starting to think she might keep some other things too.*
