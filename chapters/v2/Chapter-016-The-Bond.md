# Chapter 16 — The Bond

Three days later, I was still in the palace.

I told myself it was practical — the review was ongoing, my testimony might be needed, the formal recognition of the Vael history required documentation that I was the only one who could provide. All of this was true. None of it was the whole reason.

The whole reason was standing in the garden at dusk, which was where I found him on the third evening, which was where he apparently still went even though the reason he'd gone there alone for three years had changed.

*"You're still here,"* he said, when he heard me on the path.

*"You're still walking in the garden,"* I said.

*"Old habit."*

*"Yes,"* I said. *"Me too."*

He looked at me. The evening light was doing the thing it did in this garden — gold, low, catching the edges of things. The bond was warm between us, the way it had been since the acceptance, steady and present and no longer something either of us was managing.

*"The title,"* I said. *"I've been thinking about it."*

*"And?"*

*"It's a good offer. Practical. It solves several problems at once."*

*"Yes,"* he said. *"It does."*

*"It also means staying in the capital. Indefinitely."*

*"Yes."*

*"Near you."*

*"Yes,"* he said. *"It does mean that."*

I looked at him. He was looking at the garden, not at me, with the careful expression he wore when he was saying something that cost him something.

*"Is that a problem?"* he asked.

*"No,"* I said. *"I'm trying to decide if it's the reason I'm considering it."*

He was quiet for a moment. *"Is it?"*

*"Partly,"* I said. *"I'm not going to lie to you about that."*

He looked at me then. The full weight of it, no deflection.

---

He crossed the garden path.

Not fast — the same deliberate way he'd crossed the room three days ago, giving me time. I didn't step back. He stopped in front of me, close enough that the bond was a warmth between us, close enough that I could feel his breath.

*"I've been thinking about something,"* he said.

*"What?"*

*"The rejection."* His voice was even. *"I said four words. I didn't explain. I didn't—"* He stopped. *"I should have told you why."*

*"You were protecting me,"* I said. *"I figured it out."*

*"You shouldn't have had to figure it out."*

*"No,"* I agreed. *"I shouldn't have."*

He looked at me for a long moment. *"I'm sorry,"* he said. *"For the four words. For the way I said them."*

I hadn't expected that. I'd expected justification, or explanation, or the careful political framing he used for everything. Not *I'm sorry.*

*"Accepted,"* I said.

Something shifted in his expression — the not-quite-smile, closer to real than I'd ever seen it.

*"That word,"* he said.

*"Yes,"* I said. *"I know."*

He raised his hand and touched my face again — his palm against my jaw, the same careful way as before, like something he'd been not-doing for a long time and was now allowing himself to do. The bond flared warm and steady, not the desperate pull of the Moon Ball but something deeper, something that had been building for three weeks and had finally been given permission to exist.

I put my hand over his.

We stood like that for a moment — his palm on my jaw, my hand over his, the garden quiet around us, the bond warm between us.

*"The title,"* I said.

*"Yes?"*

*"I'll take it."*

The real smile. I'd been waiting to see it since the Moon Ball, and it was worth the wait — it changed his whole face, made him look younger, made him look like someone who had been tired for a long time and had just been told he could stop.

*"Good,"* he said.

He didn't move his hand. I didn't move mine.

The evening light went gold around us, and the bond settled into something that felt, for the first time, like it was exactly where it was supposed to be.

---

*She'd come to the Moon Ball to carry documents and stand at the back. She was leaving with a title, a bond, and a man who smiled like he'd forgotten how.*

*She kept the hairpin. She always would.*
