# Chapter 1 — The Moon Ball

It hit me like a fist.

No warning. No slow warmth. No gentle pull.

Just — impact. Sternum. Like something had been thrown at me from across the room.

My wolf went from silence to *screaming* in under a second.

I knew what it was.

Everyone in the room knew what it was.

The air changed. The nearest delegates stepped back. Someone made a small sound.

I looked up.

The Alpha King was already looking at me.

Not at the room. Not at the delegation.

At *me.*

Sera Voss. Border pack. Lowest rank. Standing at the back with a stack of documents and nowhere to go.

The bond pulled hard. My knees wanted to buckle. I locked them.

I did not look away.

Neither did he.

The whole room held its breath.

---

He walked toward me.

The crowd parted. Of course it did.

He was tall. Dark eyes. The kind of face that had learned to give nothing away and had been practicing for years.

He stopped two feet from me.

Close enough that the bond went from a fist to a full-body pressure.

Heat up my sternum. Into my throat. My pulse loud in my ears.

His jaw was set.

Something moved in his expression — there, then gone.

He looked at me for three seconds.

Then he said: *"I reject this bond."*

Four words.

No eye contact when he said them.

He was already turning away.

The room exhaled.

I stood there with the documents in my hands and the bond still *burning* — rejected doesn't mean gone, I knew that — and I thought:

*Fine.*

My wolf did not think fine.

*I know,* I told her.

---

Lord Cassius Hale found me before I reached the door.

The King's uncle. Lord Chancellor. The man who ran everything while the King sat on his dais and looked at nothing.

He smiled at me like we were old friends.

*"Miss Voss,"* he said.

I stopped.

He knew my name.

I was the lowest-ranking member of the delegation. I carried documents. I stood at the back. There was no reason — no political reason, no social reason — for this man to know my name.

I filed it.

*"A difficult evening,"* he said. *"The King's rejection was abrupt. I apologize on his behalf."*

*"No apology necessary."*

*"Still."* He produced an envelope from his jacket. Smoothly. Like it had been there all along. *"Transport back to the border. And something to ease the inconvenience."*

He held it out.

I took it.

I opened it right there, in front of him, and counted the coins.

He watched me count. His expression didn't change.

*"Forty-three,"* I said. *"Thank you. That's very kind."*

His shoulders dropped — barely, a millimeter.

Not relief that I was leaving.

Relief that I was going exactly where he'd planned.

I smiled at him.

He smiled back.

I walked out the door.

---

In the carriage, I sat with the envelope on my knee and thought about two things.

Forty-three coins. Not forty. Not fifty.

*Calculated.*

And: he knew my name before we met.

The bond pulled northwest, steady, patient.

My wolf was not patient.

*I know,* I told her. *But we're not going back.*

I looked out the window at the dark road.

Cassius had prepared this envelope before the Moon Ball began.

Before the bond was identified.

Before he could have known I would matter.

*Why did he know I would matter?*

---

*She took his money.*

*She just didn't take his exit.*
